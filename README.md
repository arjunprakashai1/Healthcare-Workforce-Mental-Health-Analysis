# Healthcare Workforce Mental Health Analysis

## Overview

This Tableau dashboard provides a comprehensive analysis of mental health indicators within the healthcare workforce. The visualization explores key metrics related to employee stress, burnout, job satisfaction, and workplace factors that impact healthcare workers' well-being.

![Mental Health Analysis](/Visuals/Mental_health_analysis_dashboard.PNG)

## Dashboard Components

### Key Performance Indicators

- **Average Stress Level**: 7.33/10 - indicating high stress across the healthcare workforce
- **Average Job Satisfaction**: 2.2/5 - revealing moderate satisfaction levels among employees

### Visualizations

#### 1. Employee Support Systems
- **EAP Access**: Distribution of employees with access to Employee Assistance Programs (71.88% have access)
- **Turnover Intention**: Percentage of employees considering leaving their positions (66.70% have turnover intentions)

#### 2. Burnout Frequency by Departments
Stacked bar chart showing burnout frequency across nine departments:
- General Medicine (highest employee count)
- Administration
- Assisted Living
- ICU
- Laboratory
- Outpatient Services
- Pediatrics
- Radiology
- Specialty Care

Burnout levels are categorized as: Never, Occasionally, and Often

#### 3. Workplace Factors Impact
Analysis of employees affected by various workplace factors:
- Heavy Workload (2,138 employees - highest impact)
- Poor Work Environment (1,122 employees)
- Career Stagnation (531 employees)
- Safety Concerns (415 employees)
- Work-Life Imbalance (340 employees)
- Emotional Demands (223 employees)
- Unclear Job Expectations (146 employees)
- Job Insecurity (85 employees)

#### 4. Departmental Workload Analysis
Horizontal bar chart identifying departments with the highest workload counts:
- General Medicine: 796
- ICU: 343
- Assisted Living: 259
- Outpatient Services: 213
- General Practice: 202
- Specialty Care: 150
- Pediatrics: 128
- Radiology: 47

## Key Insights

1. **High Stress Environment**: With an average stress level of 7.33/10, the healthcare workforce operates under significant pressure
2. **Retention Risk**: Two-thirds of employees have turnover intentions, indicating potential workforce stability issues
3. **Workload Concerns**: Heavy workload is the most significant workplace factor affecting over 2,000 employees
4. **Department Variations**: General Medicine bears the highest workload burden, followed by ICU departments

## Data Source

The dataset used for this analysis was obtained from [Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/). It includes comprehensive information about healthcare workers across multiple departments, covering stress levels, job satisfaction, workplace factors, and burnout metrics.

## Technical Details

- **Visualization Tool**: Tableau
- **Dashboard Type**: Interactive analytics dashboard
- **Primary Metrics**: Stress levels, job satisfaction, burnout frequency, workplace factors
- **Departments Analyzed**: 9 healthcare departments


## Recommendations for Action

Based on the dashboard insights, organizations should consider:
1. Implementing workload management strategies, particularly in General Medicine and ICU
2. Enhancing EAP utilization among the 28.12% without access
3. Addressing retention risks through targeted interventions
4. Developing department-specific support programs based on burnout patterns
5. Creating comprehensive wellness initiatives targeting the primary workplace stressors
